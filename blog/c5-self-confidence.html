What is the relationship between mindfulness and self-awareness?

They follow the same principle, if you increase your mindfulness (the work and goals that are being set, the time etc.) the more self-aware you become of your body, emotions and actions. It becomes easier to control them, but more importantly, respond to them therefore reserving the chance of them exhausting you.

“Self-awareness … is a neutral mode that maintains self-reflectiveness even in the midst of turbulent emotions.” — Daniel Goleman

“Mindfulness means paying attention in a particular way: on purpose, in the present moment, and non-judgmentally.” — Jon Kabat-Zinn

What did you take away from the "My Emotions are Not Me" section?

A common misconception occurs when many people make themselves and emotions as one. This is a very subtle thought that is reflected in our personality and over time it becomes a habit that can’t be avoided and our mind captures it as a principle.

It can’t be avoided until mindfulness or self-awareness comes into play. That’s when the emotions are not persons but feelings, and we are just us. We FEEL them but we AREN’T them (them being emotions).
