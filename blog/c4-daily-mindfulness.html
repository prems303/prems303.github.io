What did you learn from your 'mindfulness conversation'? How did the other person find it?

It is easy to be mindful while talking to others, but one can get distracted when others are talking about something you don’t understand or don’t have a part in, although you honestly mean good and want to listen sometimes the mind can be too distracted, and float away in a few seconds – “Dipping”. To avoid this we just need to be aware of the speaker and try not to judge them.

“The most precious gift we can offer others is our presence. When mindfulness embraces those we love, they will bloom like flowers.” - Thich Nhat Hanh

Which of the three suggested techniques will you use to sustain your practice?

Bringing ‘sukha’ (technical translation: Non-energetic joy) goes with the idea of seeing positivity in everything we do. If you bring a happy mood it’s easier to become calm and relaxed therefore letting you reach a state of relaxed-but-calm self, much faster.

“Have expectations before meditation, but have no expectation during meditation.” – Alan Wallace

Any other musings?

Happiness can be passed down through the work being done and the positivity you bring towards it.
