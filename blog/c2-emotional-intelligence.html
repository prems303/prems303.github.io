What learnings did you take from the chapter?

Mindfullness:Allows you to voluntarily bring your mind into a state that is calm, clear and focused. These include sitting and walking meditation, interactive exercises and mindful conversation.

Self Awareness:Is a process that makes you understand the role of EI (emotional intelligence) in managing unpleasant emotions. We become aware of our emotions, learn accurate self-assessment and develop self-confidence.

Self Regulation:Self-regulation builds upon self-awareness. With awareness you can identify and work with emotional "triggers" and other emotionally difficult situations.

Outcomes:The outcome of this will create the capacity for emotional self-mastery, develop inner strength for difficult situations that test your balance integrity and decrease your discomfort for disruptive change.

Empathy:You can develop useful mental habits and listening skills that help you increase empathy to better understand and work more skillfully with others.

The outcomes are strengthen your ability to understand others' feelings and perspective, develop the mental habits of kindness, goodwill and perspective which develops relationships based on trust and kindness. You can also transform conflict situations into uderstanding by listening with empathy.

How did you find the two minutes of mindfullness?

Taking two minutes to relax and focus will help reduce stress and anxiety, boost working memory, improve focus, decrease emotional reactivity, lessen depression levels.
